Free hosting decision — representative output

The right lens is commercial use on the free tier, not raw features.

- Vercel (Hobby): commercial use is prohibited on the free tier — Pro ($20/mo) required.
- GitHub Pages: prohibits e-commerce / SaaS.
- Render: commercial use allowed, but the free Postgres expires after ~30 days and free services sleep after 15 min (cold start).
- Netlify: commercial use allowed; hard-capped so no surprise bills.
- Cloudflare: commercial use allowed, no expiry, unlimited static bandwidth.

Recommendation: Cloudflare. Build SMOALD here. One gotcha — a Next.js app deploys on Workers via the OpenNext adapter, and the free Worker size cap is 3 MiB.

Big companies mostly started on cheap/free infra (early AWS) and scaled only once revenue justified it.
