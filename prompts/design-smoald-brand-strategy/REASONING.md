# Reasoning: Design the SMOALD Brand Strategy

This document captures the thinking behind the prompt — how a sprawling "empire" idea got pruned into one shippable brand.

## Goal

Settle SMOALD's brand structure before building anything — decide whether it's one company or many, how the divisions fit, what to name them, and where my freelancer portfolio lives — so the website reflects a deliberate brand instead of a guess.

## Iteration history

I worked this out over several rounds:

- **v1** — Sketched the full Virgin-style empire: every possible division (Tech, AI, Commerce, Learn, Wear, Living, Store) under one parent.
- **v2** — Started merging the overlaps. A client paying me to build an e-commerce store is buying *development*, so Commerce folds into the build hub; and in 2026 you can't build modern tech without AI, so Tech + AI merge too. Wear + Living + Store are all "selling products to consumers" — one Store hub. That collapsed six divisions into three: **AI, Learn, Store**.
- **v3** — Named the build hub. Tech vs Dev vs AI → **AI**, because my focus is AI and it commands higher rates; and decided the portfolio belongs *under* SMOALD, not on a separate site.

## Failure modes the final version handles

- **The hollow-empire trap** — six polished divisions with nothing shipped reads as fake and pulls energy away from landing actual work. The fix: build the founder brand now (one hub that gets me hired), grow into the holding-company structure later, when the divisions genuinely exist.
- **The "freelancer asking for a favour" problem** — a separate portfolio site makes me look like one more freelancer. Putting it under SMOALD means clients meet a *founder*.
- **Naming drift** — "Dev" undersells, "Tech" is generic; "AI" matches the actual focus and filters for higher-value clients.

## Outcome

A clean one-page brand model — SMOALD (parent) → AI / Learn / Store (mini-hubs) → spokes — with the portfolio at `/ai/hire`. This became the brief that the build prompt turned into the live site, and the borrow-selectively design direction (Virgin's story, Anthropic's restraint) followed from it.

## What I'd change next

Revisit per-division domains (e.g. `smoaldstore.com`) only once a division is actually earning — same "infrastructure follows traction" rule applied to branding.
